Chinese Calligraphy / New Year Couplets


Chinese Calligraphy is one kind of artistic expressions of human language in a tangible form. The genres of painting and calligraphy developed simultaneously, sharing identical tools—namely, brush and ink.

I started practicing both since high school, and developed my own style. In recent years, I've been focusing on applying both skills to write (and paint) modern Chinese New Year Couplets, which can be either a single character, a pair of two complementary poetic lines, or multiple characters being creatively combined into one in artistic forms. Couplets convey maxims or best wishes for the coming year, written on special red papers.

I love writing New Year Couplets for bringing happiness and wishes for family and friends. And some of them make "customized order" for the kind of wishes they want! ;-) Here are 11 of my recent works.





春 Spring


"Spring" is one of the most popular single characters for couplets. It means the best wishes for a rich and bountiful spring.


福 Luck


"Luck" is another self-explanatory popular character. Wishing you the best of luck in the coming year.




四季平安


It's a combination of 4 characters in the form of a single character. This wishes you a safe and peaceful year ahead.

While "四季" means "four seasons", "平安" means "safe and sound".




招財進寶


This is a special combination of 4 characters, since the characters share parts of themselves with each other. It helps you get fortune and treasure.

招 = Attract, 財 = Fortune, 進 = Receive, 寶 = Treasure.




Year of the Horse


This is for the year of horse according to the Chinese Zodiac. People likes horses for the impression of independence, integrity, enthusiasm and energy.




柿柿如意


This illustrates the popular and precious fruit in East Asia - Persimmon. It's pronouced 柿 (Shi), same as "everything" 事 (Shi), and 如意 = "as you wish".

Therefore, it symbolizes " Best of luck on everything (as you wish)!"

p.s. I was using golden ink.




恭賀新禧

吉祥如意


A 4-character pair, for congratuating you a happy new year / best of luck / and everything goes well as you wish .

The right one is the first half, and the left one is the second half.




羊肥馬壯

國富民豐


Another 4-character pair. The first half wishes your livestock grows well (" sheeps become fatter and horses get stronger"), the second half wishes " the nation becomes richer and the public gets more prosperous".




五羊爭獻瑞

萬馬喜留春


A 5-character pair. This one is especially for sending the Year of the Horse away and welcoming the upcoming Year of the Goat. It says that many goats are eager to bring blessings, and horses are happy to welcome the new spring.




平安即是全家福
和氣可生四季春

A 7-character pair. It states that (each individual) being safe and sound is already the best blessing for the family. And being gentle with each other can make each season as nice as the spring.
