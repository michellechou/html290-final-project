Project: EZTABLE Red Envelope


The innovative user referral program of EZTABLE, which successfully acquired 1% of the online population per month. Also a social product for diners to engage with family and friends.

Challenge


EZTABLE is a table-reservation service serving dinners in Asia. Theoretically speaking, every citizen could be our user, since everyone goes to a restaurant from time to time. So, I took on the challenge to scale our user acquisition efforts as the product manager & designer.

Before joining product team, I was Head of Customer Success, and I found that users who were introduced to our service by family or friends have 2x better conversion rate for making prepaid reservations, and have 30% better satisfaction rate. Also, well-designed referral programs proved to be successful in many industry-leading companies like Facebook and Airbnb. Therefore, I persuaded the CEO to approve resources for building a referral program. And my next challenge is - "What would be a appealing referral program for bother EZTABLE members & their family and friends?"

High-level Goals:

  • Provide incentive
  • Fun like a game
  • Encouraging to spread out
  • Interactive and intuitive


Design Process


User Research → Persona → Brainstorming → Ideation Decisions → User Flow (IA) → Wireframe → Prototype → User Testing



Solution & Results


We designed and built “EZTABLE Red Envelope” referral program, utilizing the monetary social gift culture in East and Southeast Asia, since it was approaching the holiday seasons of December to February (lunar new year). Every user has a customized unique red envelope online page, with his/her name written as the “giver”, and he/she can invite family and friends (“receiver”) to get to know EZTABLE and draw dining credit lucky draw there, and enjoy catching up on a dining table.

Users can find the referral program on our iOS, Android, mobile web, and websites, and share the link of his/her own red envelope page (referral link) to any of their family and friends. Since the amount of dining credit received varies, and people can see how much credits others received before they decide to sign up and draw the lucky draw, the curiosity to try their luck drew them to do so immediately. (One person can only draw the lucky draw once in the lifetime.) Furthermore, new users can redeem the credits fully on the reservation made on the platform, and the referrer would receive dining credits rebate for referring new users to reserve tables.

In addition, The red envelope link was imbedded when a user shares the reservation information to the dining companions. Dining is a social behavior that engage others, and while experiencing an EZTABLE dining experience, it's the perfect time to refer them to the service. And a red envelope sharing / dining credits earning activity proved to be just in place!


After launching it on Dec. 1, 2016, I analysed the product performance, revised the design, and rapidly launched the 2nd iteration in 14 days.
Focused on these improvements:

  1. For givers:
    (1) Emphasize on the most effective ways to send out red envelopes and refer/convert new users.
    (2) Encourage them to keep referring more people by displaying the number of the referred on a progress bar.

  2. For receivers: Emphasize on the steps to complete the lucky draw. Visible status bar.

  3. Turn each receiver into a giver as well: Guide them to intuitively give red envelopes to others after receiving theirs. Referral goes on.

  4. Co-work with tech & lifestyle influencers to give red envelope to their followers. (For example: Ms. Kai-Fu Lee. The rebate goes to charity.)

Iteration Result:

# of Users Referring New Users: 225% growth
# of New User Acquired: 300% growth

Overall Result:

Acquired 1% of the online population per month
(Reference: If it's the U.S. market, 1% online population equals to 3 million people.)
