Project: EZTABLE Red Envelope


I designed the innovative user referral program of EZTABLE, which successfully acquired 1% of the online population per month. Also a social product for diners to engage with family and friends, aligning with the monetary social gift culture of red envelopes in East and Southeast Asia.

EZTABLE is the Asia-leading table reservation service I worked for, funded and coached by Dr. Clayton Christensen who coined Disruptive Innovation theory.

My Role

I owned the product vision and led the UX/UI design & development teamwork to launch on time.

Challenge


EZTABLE is a table-reservation service serving dinners in Asia. Theoretically speaking, every citizen could be our user, since everyone goes to a restaurant from time to time. So, I took on the challenge to scale our user acquisition efforts as the product manager & designer.

Before joining product team, I was Head of Customer Success, and I found that users who were introduced to our service by family or friends have 2x better conversion rate for making prepaid reservations, and have 30% better satisfaction rate. Also, well-designed referral programs proved to be successful in many industry-leading companies like Facebook and Airbnb. Therefore, I persuaded the CEO to approve resources for building a referral program. And my next challenge is - "What would be a appealing referral program for bother EZTABLE members & their family and friends?"

High-level Goals:

  • Provide incentive
  • Fun like a game
  • Encouraging to engage as many people to go viral
  • Interactive and intuitive

Design Outcome


{v1.0 Product}

We designed and built “EZTABLE Red Envelope” referral program, utilizing the monetary social gift culture in East and Southeast Asia, since it was approaching the holiday seasons of December to February (lunar new year). Every user has a customized unique red envelope online page, with his/her name written as the “giver”, and he/she can invite family and friends (“receiver”) to get to know EZTABLE and draw dining credit lucky draw there, and enjoy catching up on a dining table.

Users can find the referral program on our iOS app, Android app, mobile website, and desktop website, and share his/her own red envelope page link (referral link) to others. Since the amount of dining credit varies each time, and people can see how much credits others received before they decide to sign up and draw the lucky draw, the motivation to try their luck and compare with others drew them to draw immediately. (One person can only draw the lucky draw once in the lifetime.) Furthermore, in order to spend the credits, they would give the prepaid reservation a try. New users can redeem the credits fully on the reservation made on the platform, and the referrer would also receive dining credit rebate for referring new users to reserve tables.

In addition, the red envelope link was imbedded when a user shares the reservation information to the dining companions. Dining is a social behavior that people engage with others, and while they experience an "EZTABLE dining experience" together, it's the perfect time for the reservation maker to refer others to the service. And a red envelope sharing / dining credits earning activity proved to be just in place!

{v2.0 Iteration}

After launching the product on Dec. 1, 2016, I analysed the product performance, revised the design, and rapidly launched the 2nd iteration in 14 days. Focused on these improvements:

  1. For givers: Emphasize on the most effective ways to send out red envelopes and refer/convert new users.
  2. For givers: Encourage them to keep referring more people by displaying the number of the referred on a progress bar.
  3. For receivers: Emphasize on the steps to complete the lucky draw. Visible status bar.
  4. Turn each receiver into a giver as well: Guide them to intuitively give red envelopes to others after receiving theirs. Referral goes on.
  5. Co-work with tech & lifestyle influencers to give red envelope to their followers. (For example: Ms. Kai-Fu Lee. The rebate goes to charity.)

User Research


Having the statement of challenge "What would be a appealing referral program for bother EZTABLE members & their family and friends?" and high-level goals (1) Provide incentive (2) Fun like a game (3) Encouraging to engage as many people to go viral (4) Interactive and intuitive, I set up 4 research topics and assigned them to one team member each. I mentored the researches, also discussed with the team to conclude the insights we learned. In total we interviewed at least 15 people for each topics, and conducted competitive analysis for the 3rd and 4th one.

Here are the research topics and top highlights of the findings:


What stops satisfied users from referring their family or friends to our service? If they've tried, did they run into any difficulties?

  • Explaining the service and guiding them to sign up could be tiring. It would be easier if there's an intuitive engaging/onboarding process, and in the form that's easy to share.
  • There's no benefits for themseleves to keep pushing the conversion rate of their recommendations.
  • There's entry barrier for people to make prepaid reservations though they can enjoy exclusive discounts, therefore their family or friends might want to stick to making reservation by phone.


Why do some diners who frequently dine out do not adopt our service?

  • Some heard about us and are quite interested, but there's no incentive so they often forget about giving it a try.

  • Some make decisions highly depending on the word-of-mouth. They might briefly heard about us, but there's no close friends recommending it in strong terms.

  • Some seldom use oneline service and never heard about us.


Why are people motivated to participate in the referral programs of similar on-demand services (ex: Airbnb, Uber)?

  • Monetary incentive for both sides.

  • Intuitive and user-friendly referring process.
    Ex: The engaging messages and links are pre-set for sharing to social media.

  • Customized.
    Ex: included the referee's name on the page titles, customized invite code.


Any interesting product feature examples that stimulate social interactions between people? What are their special elements?

WeChat Red Envelope: A social feature in WeChat app, where lucky money is given by users to their family and friends as gifts.

  • Users can set up a total cash amount and the number of lucky draw opportunities for each electornic red envelope. Ex: total $50 for a limited 10 people in a 30-people group, and the amount varies for each draw. The randomness of the amount is the key of the fun because people got to chat about their luck.
  • Users love to try their luck, so they keep raising new rounds of giving red envelopes to each others. It largely fosters social interactions between people in the app.
  • It's especially fun to play with bigger groups, therefore it goes viral quickly. Each member's single effort of giving out a red envelope got to engage multiple users.
  • After receiving some amount of e-money, people want to spend them, therefore they make transactions in the app.
