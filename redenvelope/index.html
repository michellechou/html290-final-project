Project: EZTABLE Red Envelope


The innovative user referral program of EZTABLE, which successfully acquired 1% of the online population per month. Also a social product for diners to engage with family and friends.

EZTABLE is the Asia-leading table reservation service I worked for, funded and coached by Dr. Clayton Christensen who coined Disruptive Innovation theory.

My Role:

I owned the vision of this product and led the design & development work to launch on time. I mainly work solo for the design, apart from co-working with a visual designer for the final visualizations.

Timeline:

1st Iteration: [ Design: 4 weeks ] [ Development -> QA -> Launch: 3 weeks ]
2nd Iteration: [ Design: 1 week ] [ Development -> QA -> Launch: 1 week ]



Challenge


EZTABLE is a table-reservation service serving dinners in Asia. Theoretically speaking, every citizen could be our user, since everyone goes to a restaurant from time to time. So, I took on the challenge to scale our user acquisition efforts as the product manager & designer.

Before joining product team, I was Head of Customer Success, and I found that users who were introduced to our service by family or friends have 2x better conversion rate for making prepaid reservations, and have 30% better satisfaction rate. Also, well-designed referral programs proved to be successful in many industry-leading companies like Facebook and Airbnb. Therefore, I persuaded the CEO to approve resources for building a referral program. And my next challenge is - "What would be a appealing referral program for bother EZTABLE members & their family and friends?"

High-level Goals:

  • Provide incentive
  • Fun like a game
  • Encouraging to spread out
  • Interactive and intuitive


Solution & Results


We designed and built “EZTABLE Red Envelope” referral program, utilizing the monetary social gift culture in East and Southeast Asia, since it was approaching the holiday seasons of December to February (lunar new year). Every user has a customized unique red envelope online page, with his/her name written as the “giver”, and he/she can invite family and friends (“receiver”) to get to know EZTABLE and draw dining credit lottery there, and enjoy catching up on a dining table.

The red envelope was imbedded when a user shares the reservation information to the dining companions. Users can also send the links freely to any of their family and friends. Since the amount of dining credit received varied each time, and people can see how much credits others received before they decide to sign up and draw the lottery, the curiosity to figure how lucky they are drew them to do so immediately. Furthermore, new users can redeem the credits fully on the reservation made on the platform, and the referrer would receive dining credits rebate for referring new users to reserve tables.


After launching it on Dec. 1, 2016, I observed the referral performance, re-designed, and rapidly launched the 2nd iteration in 14 days.
Focused on these improvements:

  1. For givers: Emphasize on the most effective ways to send out red envelopes and refer/convert new users.
  2. For givers: Encourage them to keep referring more people by displaying the number of the referred on a progress bar.
  3. For receivers: Emphasize on the steps to complete the lottery draw. Visible status bar.
  4. Turn receivers into givers as well: Encourage/Guide them to initiate giving red envelope to other as well after they receive theirs.
  5. Co-work with influencers to give red envelope to their followers. (For example: Ms. Kai-Fu Lee. The rebate goes to charity.)

Iteration Result:

# of Users Referring New Users: 225% growth
# of New User Acquired: 300% growth

Overall Result:

Acquired 1% of the online population per month
(Reference: If it’s in the US market, 1% online population equals to 3 million people.)
