Project: $plitting


I designed "$plitting", an app for groups to constantly split multiple bills and keep a running balance of who owes whom how much. Users can jot down expenses and split them within a click, and it will calculate and simplify mutual debts automatically. Make the tasks hassle-free.

Designed for roommates to split household bills, travel buddies to figure out trip costs, and friends who often spot cash for each other. Make sure that everyone gets paid back.

My Role

This is my solo passion project.
I owned the product vision, designed UX & UI, and created prototypes.
I plan to have the app developed in 2018.
Time Frame

2 weeks


Challenge


When having group activities, people often suffer from dealing with multiple shared bills and sorting out unpaid debts between each other.
It could be a serious problem for people who spot cash first, but in the end never get paid back correctly.



Design Process


User Research → Persona → User Story → Information Architecture → User Flow → Wireframe → User Testing → Design Iteration → Hi-Fi Prototype



User Research & Persona


I interviewed 12 people about their experiences constantly splitting multiple bills with certain groups, no matter how long the timespans are.
Then, I developed 3 personas to help guide my designs.



User Story


Based on the persona and the observations of these potential users, I started to brainstorm about how to tackle their pains.
I compiled my ideas into user stories, as following:

  1. As the initiator to split bills with my groups with an app, I want to create groups in the app so that we can record shared bills of the group.
  2. As the initiator, I want to sign up with my Facebook account so that I can always log in within a click without remembering a new set of account name and password.
  3. As the initiator, I wand to import the list of my Facebook friends, so that I can locate and invite the group members to the app / add them to the group easily, without having to type their mobile number or email manually.
  4. As a person who spot cash for the group, I want to enter only the minimum information of the to-be-splitted bills and have the app calculate for me, so that I don't have to calculate the unpaid debt of each member.
  5. As a person who spot cash for the group, I want to have multiple intuitive and useful spliting methods to choose from, so that I can bill everyone correctly with different portions within clicks.
  6. As a group member, I want to get the simplized result of the multiple mutual debts, so that we can settle up in the easiest way.
  7. As a group member, I want to be able to record bills others paid for, not only the ones I paid for, so that we can help each other to record.
  8. As a group member, I want to be notified when I was billed so that I can double confirm the correctness in time.
  9. As a group member, I want to settle up the balance on the app after we paid previous debts to each other, so that we can start over again.

User Testing & Design Iteration


After finishing wireframes, I used Proto.io to build a working prototype. I conducted user testing with 3 users for 3 scenarios (roommates sharing household expenses, travellers sharing trip costs, and friends sharing bills), in order to evaluate functions & flow and identify usability problems. During the process, these users were probed to explain their expectations and problems.

Following that, I learned about:

1. "Add friends to the app" as the first step was a bit confusing for some users. Starting directly with "Create a group" will be more intuitive, because users are driven to start using the app only when they have a specific need spliting bills with a certain group of people, so they'd expect to create a group and add people into it. They are not fond of downloading an app and firstly adding all the close friends who they might split bills with later.
→ Therefore, I simplified the flow, combined "create a group" and "add the friends" into the group creating process after considerable evaluation.

2. "Split the bill by shares" is a common need, and by adding the option it'd help more users to split the bill correctly effortlessly. (Ex: The sister of one of the roommates comes to stay for a month, and that roommate agreed to pay 2 shares for the utility.)
→ Therefore, I decided to add "by shares" as the 4th bill-spliting method, in addition to the original "split equally", "split by amount", and "split by %".
